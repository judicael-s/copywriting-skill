# X (Twitter) Formats Reference

Comprehensive reference for X copywriting: algorithm, formats, hooks, thread frameworks, viral patterns, tone, and common mistakes. Used by the social-copywriting skill.

---

## 1. Algorithm Signals

X's algorithm prioritizes content that generates conversation and earns repeat engagement. Understanding signal hierarchy shapes every copy decision.

### Ranked Signal Table

| Signal | Rank | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Replies | Highest | Conversation is the top signal — posts that generate discussion dominate |
| Retweets / Reposts | Very high | Wide distribution signal; quality reposts outweigh volume |
| Bookmarks | High | Silent quality signal — people bookmark what they intend to return to |
| Likes | Medium-High | Volume matters but weaker than replies or bookmarks |
| Dwell time | Medium-High | Time spent reading (especially threads) boosts distribution |
| Profile clicks | Medium | Indicates post drove curiosity about the author |
| Link clicks | Low-Medium | Clicks happen but external links are throttled in feed |
| Follows from tweet | High intent | Strong individual signal when a single post drives follows |

### Key Behavioral Factors

- **Early velocity (30-60 min):** Posts that get replies and bookmarks in the first 30-60 minutes get amplified disproportionately. Timing matters.
- **Threads rewarded:** Multi-tweet threads generate more total dwell time and keep people on-platform longer — algorithmically favored.
- **External links throttled:** Posts containing URLs to external sites receive reduced distribution. For links: quote-tweet your own post, or put link in reply.
- **Engagement bait detected:** "Retweet this," "Like if you agree" — the algorithm identifies and suppresses these patterns.
- **Consistency signals:** Regular posting cadence and topic focus build topical authority and improve default distribution.

## 2. Post Formats

### Single Tweets
- **Free account:** 280 characters
- **X Premium:** 25,000 characters (long-form post)
- **Default target:** Write for 280 unless the content genuinely requires more
- **Long-form posts:** Work for detailed threads collapsed into one post; still need a strong hook in first 280 chars
- **Copy rules:** One idea per tweet. Lead with the point, not the setup. Cut mercilessly.

### Threads
- **Optimal length:** 5-12 tweets (fewer feels thin; more than 15 loses most readers)
- **Pacing:** Each tweet must earn the next read. Dead middle tweets kill completion rate.
- **Numbering:** "1/" is common but optional. "🧵" at start is a signal to readers but not required.
- **Tweet 1:** Hook only — never put the insight in tweet 1. The hook creates the reason to click "show thread."
- **Last tweet:** Always a CTA. The final tweet is the highest-dwell moment of the thread.
- **Mid-thread CTAs:** Optional for threads of 10+. Place after a strong point, before continuing.
- **Internal links:** Do not link out mid-thread — it breaks flow and the algorithm penalizes it.

### Quote Tweets
- Add genuine insight — don't just re-share. Your commentary must be worth reading independently.
- Patterns: "Unpopular take on this:", "What this misses:", "Agree, and here's why it works:", "This is happening because:"
- Use to ride trending conversations with your own perspective.

### Replies as Content
- Substantive replies to high-engagement posts are a distribution hack — they appear in feeds.
- Write replies like mini-posts: one clear point, full sentences.
- The reply earns exposure to the original poster's audience.

### Polls
- Must be genuinely debatable — not a popularity contest
- 2-4 options, each as specific as possible
- Follow up with commentary on results — the follow-up gets the second engagement spike
- Best for: market research, validating assumptions, driving conversation

## 3. Character Economy

On X, every character is a decision. These techniques tighten copy without losing meaning.

### Compression Techniques

| Technique | Before | After | Chars saved |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cut adverbs | "really important to note that" | "important:" | ~25 |
| Cut filler phrases | "In my experience, I have found that" | "I've found:" | ~25 |
| Use numerals | "three things" | "3 things" | 3 |
| Dash over conjunction | "it's simple and it works" | "it's simple — it works" | 3 |
| Active voice | "mistakes were made by most people" | "most people make this mistake" | 5 |
| Cut "that" | "I know that this works" | "I know this works" | 5 |
| Front-load the point | "The reason I believe this is..." | "Here's why:" | ~20 |
| Line breaks for impact | long run-on | split into 2 lines with break | visual |

### Before / After: Character Reduction in Practice

**Before (347 characters):**
"I wanted to take a moment to share something that I think a lot of people in this space are missing, which is that the reason most people aren't seeing results from their content is not because they're posting too much or too little, it's because they haven't really figured out what their core message actually is yet."

**After (168 characters):**
"Most people's content problem isn't posting frequency.

It's that they don't have a core message.

Everything else is optimization on top of a broken foundation."

## 4. Hook Formulas

The hook is the first sentence. On X it must earn the read in under 10 words. No setup allowed.

### Formula 1: Contrarian
Challenge an assumption. Be specific about which assumption.

Examples:
- "Cold email isn't dead. Bad cold email is dead."
- "Consistency is overrated. Context is everything."

### Formula 2: Specific Result
Lead with a number and context. Precision creates credibility.

Examples:
- "I generated 47 leads last month from 6 tweets."
- "$0 in ad spend. 120K impressions. Here's the breakdown."

### Formula 3: "Most people / Nobody talks about"
Frame the post as rare or undervalued information.

Examples:
- "Most people optimize for followers. Nobody optimizes for the right followers."
- "Nobody talks about how X works for early-stage founders. Here's the truth."

### Formula 4: Open Loop
Create a gap between what the reader knows and what they need to know.

Examples:
- "There's one reason most content never converts. It's not what you think."
- "I almost gave up on this approach. Then something happened."

### Formula 5: Direct "You"
Address the reader directly with something they recognize about themselves.

Examples:
- "You're overcomplicating your marketing funnel."
- "Your content isn't underperforming. Your hook is."

### Formula 6: Number + Promise
Commit to a specific deliverable. The number signals structure and scanability.

Examples:
- "10 things I wish I knew before building my first audience."
- "3 frameworks that changed how I write copy. (All stolen from books you haven't read.)"

### Formula 7: Story Open
Drop into a scene. No preamble. Immediate.

Examples:
- "Three weeks ago I was about to delete my account."
- "My first client paid me $200. The second paid $12,000. Same work."

### Formula 8: Hot Take
Strong opinion stated plainly. No hedging.

Examples:
- "Threads are a trap if you don't have a single-tweet game."
- "Productivity content is the junk food of professional development."

### Formula 9: Unpopular Opinion (with warning)
Works when the opinion is genuine and defensible. Overused on the platform — use with substance behind it.

Examples:
- "Unpopular opinion: most 'build in public' accounts are marketing without the self-awareness."
- "Unpopular opinion: your niche doesn't need another newsletter."

Warning: If you can't defend the opinion in a thread, don't post it as a hook.

### Formula 10: List Tease
Hint at a structured list without delivering it immediately.

Examples:
- "The 5 mistakes I see on every founder's X profile: (a thread)"
- "Phrases that are quietly destroying your credibility online:"

### Choosing a Hook Formula

Match formula to goal:
- **Highest engagement:** Contrarian and Hot Take (drive quote-tweets and debate)
- **Highest follows:** Specific Result and Number + Promise (signal value, attract followers)
- **Best for threads:** Story and Open Loop (create tension that carries through multiple tweets)
- **Use sparingly:** Unpopular Opinion and Hot Take are similar — pick one per week maximum. Overuse erodes credibility.

## 5. Thread Frameworks

### Educational Thread
Best for: how-tos, frameworks, skill breakdowns

```
Tweet 1:  Hook — [problem or promise that makes people want to learn]
Tweet 2:  Why it matters — [stakes, context, why this is worth their time]
Tweets 3-8: Step-by-step — [one concrete step or point per tweet]
Tweet 9:  Recap — [summary of the key takeaway in 1-2 sentences]
Tweet 10: CTA — [follow, bookmark, DM, link to resource]
```

### Storytelling Thread
Best for: personal narrative, case study, lessons learned

```
Tweet 1:  Hook — [end state or most dramatic moment — then rewind]
Tweet 2:  Setup — [where you were, what you were trying to do]
Tweets 3-7: Rising action — [events, decisions, tensions, setbacks]
Tweet 8:  Resolution — [what happened, outcome]
Tweet 9:  Lesson — [the insight extracted from the story]
Tweet 10: CTA — [what the reader should do with this]
```

### Listicle Thread
Best for: tips, tools, resources, observations

```
Tweet 1:  Hook — [number] + [promise of what the list delivers]
Tweets 2-9: One item per tweet — [name the thing → explain it in 2-3 sentences]
Tweet 10: CTA — [save this, follow for more, reply with yours]
```

### Case Study Thread
Best for: client results, experiments, market analysis

```
Tweet 1:  Hook — lead with the result ("Here's how [person/company] went from X to Y")
Tweet 2:  Context — who, what, starting point
Tweets 3-8: Tactics — what was done, in what order, with what results
Tweet 9:  Takeaway — what this means for the reader
Tweet 10: CTA — [what the reader should do next]
```

### CTA Placement Rules
- **Never in tweet 1.** The hook earns clicks, not conversions.
- **Always in the last tweet.** The reader who made it to the end is the most qualified.
- **Optional mid-thread CTA** for threads of 10+: place it after a particularly strong point ("If you found this useful, bookmark the thread — more coming").

## 6. Viral Patterns

Patterns that consistently generate bookmarks, reposts, and follower growth on X.

**1. "I did X, here's what happened"**
Transparency + specificity. Works because it promises a real data point, not opinion.
Example: "I cold DM'd 100 people in 30 days. Here's what the data actually shows."

**2. Contrarian with evidence**
Takes a mainstream position and dismantles it with specifics.
Example: "Conventional wisdom says post 3x/day. I posted 3x/week for 6 months. Here's what happened to my reach."

**3. "Everything I know about X"**
Positions the thread as a complete resource. Very high save rate.
Example: "Everything I know about writing hooks that convert: (12 years of copywriting condensed)"

**4. Specific numbers / transparency**
Raw metrics shared openly. Revenue, follower counts, conversion rates — real numbers.
Example: "Month 6 update: 3,200 followers, $4,800 from X, 14 inbound leads. Full breakdown:"

**5. "Steal my [framework/template/process]"**
Framing useful content as something being given away creates urgency and saves.
Example: "Steal my content calendar template. It's what I use for all 4 clients. (Thread)"

**6. Prediction**
A specific, directional claim about where something is going. The more specific, the more engagement.
Example: "In 18 months, the top X accounts in every niche will be running newsletters, not threads. Here's why."

**7. "Thing nobody talks about"**
Positions the post as insider knowledge. Must actually be underreported.
Example: "The thing nobody talks about in B2B SaaS GTM: how much word-of-mouth is actually driven by Slack communities, not conferences."

**8. Before / After**
Two states, clean contrast, numbers preferred.
Example: "Before: 200 followers, posting in the void. After: 18K followers, 3 inbound deals/month. What changed: (thread)"

**9. Unpopular opinion with substance**
Strong position + genuine evidence or reasoning. Must be willing to defend it.
Example: "Unpopular opinion: most 'growth' accounts are optimizing for follower count, not business outcomes. They're different games."

**10. Curation + commentary**
Share a resource, observation, or quote — then add your specific insight. Commentary elevates the retweet.
Example: "[Quote from article] — This misses the most important factor. Here's what's actually happening:"

## 7. X Tone

### Tone Principles for X

**Directness:** Say the thing. No preamble, no throat-clearing. X rewards confidence.

**Personality:** Bland is invisible. Your voice must be recognizable in the first 5 words.

**Wit:** Not jokes — but intelligence worn lightly. A twist of phrase that earns a reread.

**Confidence + specificity:** Vague hedging destroys credibility. Strong claims with evidence create authority.

**Calibrated vulnerability:** Failures and setbacks, shared specifically and without performance, outperform success theater.

**Speed:** X reads fast. Write for skim-then-read, not read-then-reread.

### X vs. LinkedIn Tone Table

| Dimension | X | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | Low — conversational, direct | Medium — professional but human |
| Humor | Works, especially dry / deadpan | Use sparingly; self-deprecating works |
| Hot takes | Expected, rewarded | Use with care — can polarize |
| Self-deprecation | Works well | Works in moderation |
| Sentence length | Short — often 8-12 words | Medium — 10-20 words |
| Hashtags | 0-2, almost optional | 3-5, placed at end |
| External links | Throttled; minimize or use reply | Move to first comment |
| Self-promotion | Low tolerance; embed in value | Allowed more directly |
| Emotional register | Punchy, opinionated | Warm, professional, reflective |
| Length norms | 140-280 chars for standalone | 800-1,600 chars for full posts |

## 8. Bio and Profile

### Bio Formulas (160 characters)

**Formula 1: Role + Proof + Topic**
`[What you do] | [Proof point] | [What you write/talk about]`
Example: `B2B copywriter | $40M in tracked pipeline | Writing about conversion, positioning, and offers`

**Formula 2: Outcome + Audience**
`I help [audience] [achieve outcome] | [secondary proof or context]`
Example: `I help founders write content that generates pipeline, not just followers | 12K+ in 8 months`

**Formula 3: Identity + Track Record + Promise**
`[Who you are]. [What you've built/done]. [What followers get]`
Example: `Marketer. Built 3 brands from 0 to 7 figures. Writing what I learn in public.`

**Formula 4: Contrarian / Personality**
`[Strong belief or position] | [What you do]`
Example: `Most marketing advice is wrong | Testing what actually works in B2B SaaS`

**Formula 5: Minimal / High-Status**
`[Job title or role]. [Company or context]. [Optional single proof point]`
Example: `CMO @Acmecorp. 2× exits. Writing about growth.`

### Pinned Tweet Strategy

The pinned tweet is the highest-visibility real estate on your profile. Rotate based on current priority.

**4 High-Performing Pinned Tweet Strategies:**

1. **Best thread** — Your highest-performing thread. Pins signal your best work to profile visitors.
2. **Intro tweet** — "If you're new here: I write about X. Here's my best content to start." Drives follows from profile visitors.
3. **Lead magnet** — Link to a free resource, guide, or newsletter in a reply to your own post. Drives email captures.
4. **Signature tweet** — Your single best standalone post. A statement of belief, a strong take, or a specific insight that defines your POV.

**Rotation:** Review every 2-4 weeks. Pin what's currently most relevant to your goals.

## 9. Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags on X carry far less weight than on Instagram or LinkedIn. Treat them as labels, not amplifiers.

- **0-2 per tweet** — More than that looks like spam
- **Niche > broad** — `#b2bsaas` outperforms `#marketing` for targeted reach
- **Never use hashtags in threads** — Interrupts flow; no meaningful distribution benefit
- **Event hashtags exception** — Live events, conferences, product launches: hashtags make sense and drive real reach
- **When to skip entirely:** Accounts with 5K+ followers (your audience already finds you), conversational posts, opinion posts where hashtags feel performative

## 10. Niche Guidance

### Tech Twitter
- **Tone:** Direct, opinionated, technically grounded
- **Proof types:** Code snippets, benchmarks, specific technical decisions
- **Top story type:** Migration stories ("We moved from X to Y — here's what we learned"), architecture decisions
- **Humor:** Technical in-jokes, self-deprecating about complexity
- **CTA style:** "What's your take?" / "Anyone else seen this?"
- **Thread style:** Dense, technical — readers expect depth

### Founder / Build-in-Public Twitter
- **Tone:** Transparent, metrics-forward, honest about failures
- **Proof types:** MRR, churn, CAC, screenshots of Stripe/revenue dashboards
- **Top story type:** Behind-the-scenes decisions, failures with lessons, month-in-review updates
- **Humor:** Self-deprecating, "startup reality" humor
- **CTA style:** "Roast my landing page" / "Would you use this?"
- **Thread style:** Personal narrative + data

### Marketing Twitter
- **Tone:** Confident, specific, example-heavy
- **Proof types:** Campaign results, A/B test data, conversion rates
- **Top story type:** Teardowns (ad analysis, copy breakdowns), framework threads
- **Humor:** Dry takes on industry bullshit
- **CTA style:** "What's your go-to here?" / "Save this for next campaign"
- **Thread style:** Educational + tactical — one actionable insight per tweet

### Niche Comparison Table

| Factor | Tech Twitter | Founder Twitter | Marketing Twitter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proof points | Technical specifics | Revenue / growth metrics | Campaign data / conversion |
| Story type | Migration, architecture | Transparency, failure | Teardowns, frameworks |
| Humor register | Technical, nerdy | Self-deprecating | Industry sarcasm |
| CTA tone | Peer question | Community feedback | Save / apply |
| Thread density | High | Medium | Medium-High |

## 11. Common Mistakes

**1. Leading with a link** — The first tweet in a thread or standalone post should never be a link. It's throttled. Put links in a reply to your own post.

**2. Thread padding** — Tweets that say nothing to make the thread longer. Every tweet must earn its place. Cut ruthlessly.

**3. Engagement bait** — "RT if you agree," "Like this if you've experienced this." Detected and suppressed by the algorithm.

**4. No hook** — Starting a thread with context or setup. Tweet 1 = hook only. No exceptions.

**5. Too many topics** — Posting about everything makes you known for nothing. Topical authority compounds; scatter resets it.

**6. Ignoring replies** — Replies in the first 30-60 minutes are the primary distribution lever. Not responding is leaving reach on the table.

**7. Cross-posting from LinkedIn** — LinkedIn copy pasted onto X looks like LinkedIn copy. The register is different. The formatting is different. Adapt everything.

**8. Bad self-promo ratio** — More than 15-20% promotional posts trains your audience to skip you. Earn with value; ask occasionally.

**9. Inconsistent posting** — Posting 20 times in one week and then going dark for 10 days resets your algorithmic standing. Consistent beats intense.

**10. Press release tone** — "We are thrilled to announce..." on a personal account. Write like a person.

**11. Screenshot threads** — Threads made of screenshots of text. Not indexed, not searchable, reads as low-effort.

**12. Apologizing for self-promo** — "Sorry for the promo but..." Just post it. The apology signals insecurity and makes the promo feel worse.

## 12. Templates

### Single Tweet Templates

**Observation:**
`[Specific observation]. [Why it matters / what it means for the reader]. [Optional: one-line implication]`

Example:
`The best copywriters I know read more novels than marketing books.
Voice, rhythm, and character come from literature.
The conversion tactics come later.`

**Tip:**
`[Name the thing]: [explain it in one line].
[Why it works / when to use it].
[Optional: result or example]`

Example:
`Open loop hooks: end the first sentence mid-idea.
The brain hates unresolved tension. It clicks.
Use sparingly — overuse trains people to ignore you.`

**Story Micro:**
`[Scene-setting sentence with tension].
[What happened next — one line].
[Lesson or punchline — one line]`

Example:
`My first sales call lasted 4 minutes.
The prospect said: "You're not ready."
He was right. That conversation made me $200K over the next 3 years.`

### Thread Opener Templates

**Template A — Numbered promise:**
`I [did/studied/tested/analyzed] [specific thing] for [time period].

(thread)`

**Template B — Result first:**
`[Specific impressive result].

[tweet 1 of X]`

**Template C — Contrarian:**
`[Common belief] is wrong.

I [action that proves it].

### Thread Closer Templates

**Template A — Utility CTA:**
